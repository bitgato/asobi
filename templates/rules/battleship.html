Rules

Players Two

Description

Players take turns in trying to guess the locations of the other player's ships on a grid.

Each player gets two 10 x 10 grids, labelled along the sides with letters and numbers. On the left-hand grid the player secretly places his fleet of ships.

Each player's fleet consists of the following ships:

ShipUnitsSize
Carrierx15 squares
Battleshipx14 squares
Cruiserx13 squares
Submarinex22 squares
Destroyerx21 square

Gameplay

Each player places all his units on the placement board (right for bigger and down for smaller screens). This is done by dragging a ship from the port and dropping them on the board. The ships' positions can be adjusted freely by dragging and rotating. While the ships may touch each other, they may not overlap.

When all the 7 units have been placed on the board, the player needs to confirm the placement.

The game starts when both the players have confirmed the positions of their fleet. The game creator gets the first turn to guess. To attack, the players need to click a square on the attack board (left for bigger screens and up for smaller screens.

Attacks that have landed on a ship are shown with a red cross, while the attacks that missed are shown with a blue cross. Attacks done by the player is shown on the attack board and the attacks done by the opponent is shown on the placement board. Neither player can see the other player's fleet placement.

When a player's attack destroys a ship, the ship's silhouette is shown on the attacking player's attack board. The ship's owner doesn't see any change on the attack board, but the ship on his placement board has red crosses over all the squares it occupies, indicating it has been destroyed.

The game continues till one of the player's fleet is destroyed completely.
